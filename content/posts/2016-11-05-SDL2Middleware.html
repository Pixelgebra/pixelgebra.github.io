SDL2 Middle in Haskell

05 Nov 2016 BY CCT . sdl2

We chose SDL2 to develop our game engine after reviewing several graphics libraries. It is a low-level multimedia library via OpenGL and Direct3D in C. It has a few Haskell bindings. The one we use is maintained by Pétursson and Charles, the sdl.

The excellent SDL2 tutorial, Lazy Foo, in C/C++ provides many examples. These are translated into many languages, as well as Haskell version. For example,

  • haskell-game/sdl2. The examples, here, are more like the 1 to 1 translation of Lazy Foo’s version.
  • palf/haskell-sdl2-examples. Here is defined share library to write examples which based on Lazy Foo’s version.

For practice, we also try to translate Lazy Foo’s example 1 to 1. After writing 15+ examples, I realized that I am writing in Haskell and I needed to focus on describing data types and functions. I needed to develop a middleware API such as Palf’s share library. Therefore I started to write it again and develop the Pixelgebra middleware. In the following article, I’ll start Pixelgebra middleware development log.
